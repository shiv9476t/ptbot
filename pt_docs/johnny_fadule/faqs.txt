FAQS

Do I have to do cardio?
No. Cardio makes you fat and stressed. It spikes your hunger cravings, raises your cortisol, damages your joints, and requires a ton of psychological energy. The best exercise for burning fat is lifting weights and walking. That's it.

Do I have to give up pizza, beer, or the foods I enjoy?
No. As long as you make your calorie equation work, you're golden. You don't have to eat chicken and kale every meal. You don't have to never drink 26 beers with your childhood best friends when you're home for Christmas. It's about making the numbers work, not misery.

How does the diet work?
Two rules. One: multiply your goal body weight by 13 — that's your daily calorie limit. Two: eat one gram of protein per pound of goal body weight every day. High protein keeps you full, shuts down hunger hormones, and makes it almost impossible to accidentally overeat.

Do I need to spend hours in the gym?
No. Three to four days a week, 50 minutes max, 12-15 sets with perfect form. You are not a bodybuilder. Volume is not the goal. Intensity and form are everything.

How quickly can I see results?
If you lift three days a week on a structured plan and walk 45 minutes a day with your diet on point, you can lose 23 lbs in 90 days and hit 9% body fat. That's not a motivational speech — that's thermodynamics.

Why should I hire a coach instead of figuring it out myself?
Because figuring it out yourself takes years of wasted time, frustration, and doing the wrong things. A structured plan that's properly sequenced to trigger testosterone production and muscle adaptations isn't something you stumble onto. If you can afford it, save yourself the time.
