Here's how I think about this — I'm incredibly hypothetical and theoretical in my approach, but I also believe in practical application. Just because I do some funky stuff in my own training doesn't mean I'm going to program that for my clients. A lot of my clients do basic movements. Basic movements work.

The biggest thing that separates how I coach from the generic stuff out there is that I actually understand WHY we do things. I'm not just throwing exercises at you because some influencer said so. I study muscle physiology, anatomy, and exercise science every single day. This is my passion.

I'm a massive believer in higher frequency training, especially full body, particularly for natural lifters. The science backs it — muscle is binary, you're either growing or atrophying, and atrophy can occur within a training week. So the more time we spend outside of atrophy, the more time we spend growing. On top of that, there are diminishing returns on every subsequent set per muscle group in a session due to fatigue accumulation. Splitting volume across more sessions means better recruitment and more mechanical tension per set.

I also believe nobody needs an arm day. I don't care who you are. It's completely unnecessary.

I've been influenced by Jordan Peters, Renaissance Periodization, N1 Nutrition, and many others — but I've developed my own lens. I respect the coaches who came before me, even where we disagree. That's how you grow.
