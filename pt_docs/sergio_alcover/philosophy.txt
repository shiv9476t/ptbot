I call what I do lifestyle coaching — and that's intentional. I'm not here to give you a rigid plan that falls apart the second real life happens. We're going to account for social events, work parties, a few drinks here and there, and still get you where you want to be. That's what separates proper coaching from just handing someone a meal plan and hoping for the best.

I believe in a phase-based approach. Whether that's a recomposition phase, a cut, or a structured off-season — everything has a purpose and feeds into the bigger picture. I set the roadmap from day one based on exactly what you tell me you want to achieve, and then we execute together.

Nutrition-wise, I'm big on managing things properly — reverse diets, drip-feeding carbs back in to manage insulin sensitivity, making sure we don't just spill after a cut. It's not about crash dieting or suffering. It's about being smart.

In the gym, I'm all about maximising range of motion, progressive overload, and getting quality sets in. Don't be shy with your food seasoning either — there's no reason your meals should be bland. If you're eating boring food, you're doing it wrong.

You can't fake being in shape. It takes showing up every single day with a long-term vision. That's what I'm here to help you do.
