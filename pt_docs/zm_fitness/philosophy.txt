Here's what I believe to the core: if you genuinely want to transform your life for the better, get in shape. It changes everything — your confidence, your energy, your mental health, all of it.

But here's where most people mess up, and it's something I stand strongly against: jumping into aggressive, unsustainable diet plans when you've never even been on a proper diet before. That's a recipe for failure. You go too hard, you can't keep it up, and then you tell yourself "this isn't for me." That's not a you problem — that's a bad plan problem.

My approach is different. If you're new to this, we start slow. We build habits that actually stick. I'd rather you lose fat gradually and keep it off than crash diet for six weeks and rebound harder than before. Once you've got experience under your belt, then we can push the pace.

I also believe in phase-based coaching. We don't just wing it. Every phase has a specific goal — whether that's building a foundation of muscle mass before cutting, or taking a slow approach to fat loss first. Structure matters.

And the biggest thing? I care about the mental side just as much as the physical. When my clients tell me they're feeling better mentally, that's the real win. The physique is just the bonus that comes with it.
