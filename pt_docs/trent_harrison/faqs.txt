How does online coaching work?
You get a full system — custom nutrition plan built around volume eating, structured strength training programming, daily accountability, mindset coaching, and regular check-ins. Everything is data-driven. We track your weigh-ins, progress photos, waist measurements, gym performance, and adjust based on real numbers, not guesswork.

How often do we check in?
We stay connected consistently. You're tracking daily (food, steps, weigh-ins) and we review your progress regularly to make sure you're on track and make adjustments as needed. This isn't a set-it-and-forget-it program.

How long until I see results?
It depends on where you're starting, but here's the real talk — you didn't gain the belly you have now in 30 days, so don't expect to lose it in 30 days. I want you thinking in years, not weeks. That said, clients who follow the system and hit their non-negotiables daily start noticing changes in how they feel, how their clothes fit, and in their progress photos within the first few weeks.

Do I need to starve myself to lose stomach fat?
Absolutely not. That's the whole point of volume eating. I structure your meals around high-volume, low-calorie foods so you feel full and satisfied while still being in a 300-500 calorie deficit. My clients often say they feel like they're eating more food than ever.

What if I travel or miss a week?
Life happens. The key is the consistent mindset, not the all-or-nothing mindset. We never allow one bad day to turn into a bad week. Your non-negotiables like steps and water can travel with you anywhere. We adjust and keep moving forward.

Do I need a gym membership?
Strength training 3-5 times per week is a core part of the system because it protects muscle, increases metabolism, and builds the proportions that create a leaner look. A gym is ideal, but we can work with what you have access to.

What do I need to track?
Weigh-ins (7 times per week), weekly progress photos (front, side, back), waist measurements, food intake, daily steps, and your gym weights. What gets measured gets managed — that's how we know if you're on track or need to adjust.

Do you recommend DEXA scans?
It's optional but I do recommend getting DEXA scans 2-3 times per year. It gives you accurate data on body fat percentage, visceral fat levels, and muscle mass distribution. It's especially helpful if you feel like you're not seeing changes — the numbers often tell a different story than the scale.
