Objection: I've tried online coaching before and it didn't work.
Response: I hear you, and honestly a lot of coaching out there is just templates being sent out with no real thought behind them. The way I work is different — I'm actually looking at your training, reviewing your lifts, and making changes specific to you. Objection: I can't afford a coach right now.
Response: I get it, money's real. But think about it this way — how long have you been spinning your wheels doing the same thing? If you've been stuck for months or even years, that's time you're not getting back. A good coach accelerates your progress so you're not wasting time and effort on stuff that isn't working. Shoot me a message and let's at least have a conversation about where you're at — it might be more accessible than you think.

Objection: I don't have time to follow a strict program.
Response: None of us are perfect, and that's literally my first point when I talk about making progress. You don't need to be a monk. You just need to be making the right decisions more often than not. I work with real people who have jobs, relationships, long drives, all of it. I was literally driving 7 hours before a session the other week. The programming works around your life — but you do need to commit to the basics like actually eating before you train and being consistent.

Objection: I'm not sure I'm advanced enough to need a coach.
Response: Honestly, that's probably the best time to get a coach — before you've spent years building bad habits or running yourself into the ground. If I had a coach earlier in my journey I'd probably be even further ahead. The earlier you get proper guidance, the faster you progress and the fewer mistakes you make along the way.

Objection: I don't want to gain weight or look less lean.
Response: Look, I'm going to be straight with you because that's how I am. If you want to aesthetics max and stay shredded year round, that's your choice, but you need to accept you're not going to make progress at nearly the rate you could be. I've talked about this so many times — gaining weight fuels your training, improves recovery, makes you more resistant to injuries. You always have the option to cut back down later, but with more muscle. Unless someone is paying you to look a certain way, the sacrifices you make to stay super lean are honestly not worth it when you break it down.
