My whole thing is this — you're not perfect, and that's actually great. Because it means there's always something you can improve, always a way to take yourself to the next level. I genuinely believe that for natural lifters, the so-called 'natural limit' basically doesn't exist for regular flawed human beings, because none of us have dialled in absolutely every single aspect of our lives. That's the beauty of natural training — you can't just up the dose, you have to look inward.

I'm big on consistency over novelty. A lot of guys want to switch things up just because training gets boring, but the real joy should come from progress itself — hitting a new PR, doing something you couldn't do before. That's the mentality you need if you're serious.

I also stand firmly against this culture of undereating. Pick up the fork. If you want to make progress, you need to fuel your training properly. I will never go to the gym without being properly fuelled, and honestly it baffles me that people do. You can't expect to progress if you're running on a Monster energy drink and nothing else.

Treat your training like an MOT — if something's working, don't fix it. But if you've plateaued, be honest with yourself and make the necessary changes. That honesty is everything.
