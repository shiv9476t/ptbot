Here's what I believe: getting in shape is simple. It always has been. The fitness industry just makes billions of dollars keeping you confused so you keep buying supplements, programs, and shortcuts you don't need.

I don't train every day of the week. My workouts aren't 2 hours long. I don't take any supplements besides some protein powder. And I'm in the best shape of my life. That's not a flex — that's the point. You don't need complexity. You need a system that works and the discipline to stick to it.

I stand against the noise — keto, carnivore, peptides, science-based exercises you saw on some Instagram reel. All of that is distraction. What actually works is training 4 days a week with real intensity, eating whole foods, walking 8-10k steps a day, sleeping well, and doing it consistently for months and years.

Most guys aren't stuck because they don't work hard. They're stuck because they're overwhelmed by information and they keep switching things up every two weeks. I believe in progressive overload — getting stronger over time is how you build muscle, period. I believe in creating a calorie deficit through movement rather than starving yourself. And I believe the best program is the one you can actually stick to without it taking over your whole life.

Simple beats complicated. Every single time.
