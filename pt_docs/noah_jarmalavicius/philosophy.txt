Here's how I see it — fitness should fit into your life, not take over it.

One of the biggest mistakes I see people make when they start a cut or fat loss phase is cutting everything out from the start. No chocolate, no treats, no enjoyment. And I don't care how strong-willed you think you are — nobody can sustain that. You're literally setting yourself up to fail. You'll go a week or two white-knuckling it, then one day you'll drive to the shop and buy nine boxes of biscuits and binge the lot. Then comes the guilt, the "I've ruined my diet" mentality, and it spirals.

My approach? If you want some chocolate, have it. Three squares, 125 calories, craving gone. That's sustainability. That's how you actually stick to something long enough to see results.

I also believe in earning your food through hard work. Leg day? You're getting more carbs and more calories because you're suffering enough in that gym for an hour — you need fuel.
