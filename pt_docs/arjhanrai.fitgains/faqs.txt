How does online coaching work?
We set you up with a personalised nutrition plan based on your calorie needs, body fat percentage, and the foods you actually enjoy. You also get a custom strength training programme. We track your progress through daily weigh-ins, weekly measurements, and monthly photos, and make adjustments as needed to keep you moving forward.

Do I need to give up my favourite foods?
Absolutely not. We include the foods you love on your plan — roti, chocolate, takeaways, even a couple of drinks. As long as you stay within your calorie allowance and hit your protein target, you'll get results.

How often do we check in?
We monitor your progress regularly using your daily weigh-in averages, weekly measurements, and monthly progress photos. If something needs adjusting, we'll catch it and make changes.

How long until I see results?
It depends on where you're starting from, but you'll typically be in the fat loss phase for around 6 months or longer. Our calculator gives you a realistic timeline based on your current body fat percentage and goals.

Do I need a gym membership?
Yes, strength training is a core part of the programme. You'll need access to equipment for compound lifts like squats, deadlifts, bench press, rows, and shoulder press. A proper gym is ideal.

What if I've been yo-yo dieting for years?
That's actually really common with our clients. Yo-yo dieting adds more fat cells over time, making it harder — but not impossible. Our three-phase approach (Fat Loss, Reverse Diet, Freedom Phase) is specifically designed to break that cycle so you lose the weight and actually keep it off.

What happens after I hit my goal weight?
This is where most programmes fail. After the fat loss phase, we take you through a reverse diet phase where we gradually bring your calories back up, and then into the freedom phase where you maintain your results long term without rigid tracking.

Can I still follow this if I'm vegetarian or vegan?
Yes. The principles work regardless of your dietary preference — vegetarian, vegan, pescatarian, whatever. We just need to make sure you're hitting your calories, protein, and fibre targets.
