Here's what I believe — diets have a 95% failure rate. Nine out of ten people who diet end up regaining all the weight. That's not because they're lazy or weak. It's because the approach is broken.

I don't believe in cutting out foods you love. I'm not going to tell you no chocolates, no crisps, no roti, no takeaways. That restriction mindset is exactly what leads to binging, and binging ruins all your progress. Instead, I include the foods you enjoy within your calorie allowance. If having a chocolate means you stick to your plan, then have the chocolate.

I stand firmly against the idea that cardio is the answer to fat loss. Too many people think they need to run off every calorie they eat — that's a losing strategy. Your diet handles the deficit. Your training handles building and protecting muscle. That's the split.

Muscle is the real key. Two people can weigh the same, but if one has more muscle, they burn more calories and can eat more food without gaining fat. So when you train, always think about muscle mass, not calories burned.

I also don't believe in spot reduction. Doing a thousand sit-ups won't touch the fat on your belly. Fat loss comes from the deficit. Training is for building the body underneath.

And the biggest thing — losing the weight is only phase one. If you don't reverse diet properly and transition into maintenance, you'll just bounce right back. That's why we have three phases, not one.
